How to schedule Instagram posts for multiple clients (without losing your mind)

By Nishant Singh, founder of My Digital Sevak — after scheduling for 63+ brands · Published July 10, 2026

To schedule Instagram posts for multiple clients, connect each client's account once through Meta's official API in a multi-client scheduler, plan every brand on one shared calendar, get approvals inside the same tool, and let posts auto-publish at their scheduled times. No password sharing, no logging in and out of accounts.

Here's the moment every growing agency hits: the client roster crosses what one phone can handle, and the phone-based posting system collapses. Instagram caps logged-in accounts at ten per device — and in practice the chaos starts well before the cap, because manual posting means juggling notifications, stories and DMs for every brand from one screen. I ran posting for 63+ brands at my agency, and I can tell you exactly where the manual system breaks and what to replace it with.

Why does manual posting break as clients grow?

  • The device limit is real. Ten logged-in accounts per device is Instagram's ceiling. Two phones buys you twenty accounts — and twice the chaos, because the problem was never the login count, it's one human posting for every brand by hand.
  • Passwords become a liability. Clients WhatsApp you their password, then change it, then blame you when a post doesn't go out. Password sharing also violates Instagram's terms and invites suspicious-login blocks.
  • 9 AM posts go out at 11:30. Manual posting depends on a human being free at the exact minute. Humans are in client meetings at that minute.
  • No record of what went live. When posting happens on someone's phone, your content calendar and reality drift apart within a week.

What's the right workflow for scheduling multiple client accounts?

Every serious guide — Planable's, Cloud Campaign's, ours — lands on the same core loop. The difference is in the details, so here's the version that survived contact with 63 real brands:

  1. Connect each brand once, officially. The client (or you, on a call with them) authorises Instagram + Facebook through Meta's login. Takes two minutes per brand, needs doing once, and access can be revoked anytime. Never store a password.
  2. Plan a month per client on one calendar. Batch-plan each brand's month in one sitting — reels, carousels, statics, captions, hashtags. A month of planning per client should take an afternoon, not a week.
  3. Route everything through approval before it can publish. The post physically cannot go live until the client taps approve. This one gate eliminates the "who said this could go out?" call forever.
  4. Let approved posts publish themselves. Scheduled time arrives → post goes live → status flips to Posted with the permalink. Nobody's phone is involved.
  5. Let the numbers flow back automatically. Reach and engagement sync back per post, so month-end reports assemble themselves instead of eating two days.

The month-in-a-day batching workflow

Once accounts are connected, the biggest time-saver isn't the auto-posting — it's batching. Here's the cadence that worked across our brands:

  • Day 25 of the month: plan next month's calendar per client — themes, formats, festival dates (Indian calendars live and die by festival campaigns; block Raksha Bandhan, Diwali, regional days first).
  • Days 26–28: production. Designers and editors work from the calendar, attaching creatives to the planned posts — not to WhatsApp.
  • Day 29: send the whole month for client approval in one batch. One sitting for the client, one reminder from you.
  • Day 30: schedule everything approved. Assign each client fixed time slots based on when their audience is online — a café's followers scroll at 8 PM, a B2B brand's at 11 AM — and reuse those slots every month instead of picking times post-by-post.

From the 1st, the month runs itself: posts publish on schedule, revisions trickle in on individual posts, and your team's daily posting hour disappears. New client onboarding drops into the same rhythm — connect the account once during onboarding, and they're in next month's batch.

Frequently asked questions

How many Instagram accounts can an agency manage from one phone?

Instagram allows up to ten logged-in accounts per device. Past that you're constantly logging in and out — and well before ten, manual posting means juggling notifications, stories and DMs across every account from one phone, which is where mistakes start.

Can I schedule Instagram reels and carousels for clients?

Yes — through Meta's official API, schedulers auto-publish reels, carousels and single-image posts, including captions and covers. Stories generally still require a notification-based manual step on most platforms.

Is it safe to schedule client posts with a third-party tool?

Yes, if the tool connects through Meta's official Instagram API with the client authorising access through their own login. Avoid any tool that asks to store the client's Instagram password — that violates Instagram's terms and risks account blocks.
